We All Want to Be Heard

The desire to be heard is an innate part of what makes us human

As communities grew, it became harder for each person to feel heard -- to feel valued. In today's chaotic, fast-paced world, it's nearly impossible to feel like your input makes a difference on a larger scale. This is bad for each individual and for the greater community.

Large communities struggle to engage all their members. Those that have been ignored for so long stop speaking up. Unrepresentative feedback strengthens the cycle that silences under served communities.

Voices Fall on Deaf Ears

Too often, the will of the people is ignored
Of the people, by the people, for the people

Global movements captivate people and the media's attention but action from representatives either never comes or takes months of persuasion.


There needs to be a way for people to organize their ideas and passion to create change, not ask for it. It needs to be easier for groups to advocate for themselves.

All People Deserve Equal Representation

Waiting 4 Years isn't Helping

Issues demand action now

People are powerless between elections. They have no say on the issues that are affecting them in the here and now. Poor representation and no way to do anything about it leads to burnout and frustration. There needs to be a way for people to meaningfully participate in the democratic process on a daily basis.

Representation Matters

We are committed to amplifying the voices of all people. At the same time, we want to prioritize historically under-represented communities. The power of a vote should be equal for all people - and that starts with making voting fair and easily accessible to all people.

It's time for a new way

Quae puts representation on the cutting edge

Quae utilizes modern technology to bring democracy to the 21st century. By giving everyone the opportunity and accessibility to propose solutions and verifiably vote on a daily basis, Quae eliminates the issue of having to wait for election cycles for citizens to have an impact.
